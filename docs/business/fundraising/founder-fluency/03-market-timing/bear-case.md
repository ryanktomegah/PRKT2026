## B-MKT-01 — No bank LOI signed yet

**Honest Truth:** Zero letters of intent as of today. Zero production traffic. The working demo priced a two-million eight-hundred ninety-thousand-dollar ($2,890,000) bridge at seven hundred six basis points in real time — that is the only live proof point. The RBC pilot is the gate.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "Zero LOIs. Zero production banks. The demo is the only live proof point."
2. Name the path. Three parallel vectors into RBC: RBCx (Sid Paquette) as innovation-accelerator sponsor, Transaction Banking (Derek Neldner) as the operational buyer who owns the pacs.002 pain, and the RBC AI Group (Bruce Ross, formed February 2026) as the AI-mandate channel. Sequence is specified: resign from RBC, file the provisional patent, approach as an external vendor.
3. Reframe as sequencing discipline. The absence of a signed LOI today is a direct consequence of the META-01 employment clause — approaching a pilot bank before resignation creates a chain-of-title conflict. Filing the provisional and resigning before outreach is the discipline that protects the patent priority date and the licensee's diligence review.
4. Name the milestone. First LOI signed is the resolution event. At that point, the market claim — correspondent banking stack, bank-as-borrower, not balance-sheet lender — becomes evidentiary rather than theoretical.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "We have a pipeline of early-stage conversations" (vague; admit zero)
- ❌ "LOIs are close" (no evidence base)
- ❌ "We'll have one before the round closes" (anchoring commitment to a timeline we don't control)

**Resolution Milestone:** First LOI signed — RBCx, Transaction Banking, RBC AI Group, or a non-RBC Tier 2 Canadian/European bank.

**Investor Intuition target:** "They know they have nothing signed. They've sequenced the gating risk — employment IP first, then patent, then external outreach. The absence of an LOI is a timing discipline, not a market signal."

## B-MKT-02 — Bank procurement cycles eighteen to twenty-four months

**Honest Truth:** Tier 1 bank procurement cycles for new vendors run eighteen to twenty-four months from first meeting to signed pilot contract. That is the correspondent-banking reality. LIP is a licensed platform, not a SaaS trial — the MRFA, BIC-derived governing law, and compliance warranties require full bank legal review.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "Eighteen to twenty-four months is the bank procurement cycle. That is not a number we can compress by pushing harder."
2. Reframe as structural fit. Two compression levers exist inside the bank. First, RBCx is an innovation-accelerator channel with an explicit mandate to shortcut conventional procurement for fintech pilots — Sid Paquette's team exists for exactly this. Second, parallel vectors through Transaction Banking and the RBC AI Group run independently, so the effective timeline is the fastest of three parallel processes, not the expected single-track time.
3. State the runway sizing. Seed capital at two hundred thousand dollars ($200K) per month over eighteen months is sized against the full procurement cycle, not against a best-case. The capital plan does not depend on a short pilot timeline.
4. Name the milestone. A signed pilot contract — not an LOI, not a memorandum of interest — is the resolution event. The contract triggers deployment into the bank's production environment and the first live UETR.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "We've heard of faster deals in fintech" (irrelevant to correspondent banking)
- ❌ "If RBCx moves fast we could be in production in six months" (overpromise)
- ❌ "We'll adapt the timeline" (the bank's timeline governs, not ours)

**Resolution Milestone:** First pilot contract signed — triggers production deployment.

**Investor Intuition target:** "They've built a capital plan that matches the cycle. They're not hoping it goes faster — they're sized for the real timeline and working the parallel vectors."

**Drill linkage:** Q-MKT-06 (Warm — "How does a bank actually buy LIP?") and Q-MKT-13 (Probing — "Bank procurement cycles are eighteen to twenty-four months. How do you survive that?") Both confirmed in drill.md.

## B-MKT-03 — ISO 20022 migration deadline has slipped before

**Honest Truth:** SWIFT's original ISO 20022 migration deadline was November 2022; it was extended to November 2025. Deadline slippage is a historical pattern. National regulators may extend phase-in windows further. LIP's entire "why now" argument rests on structured pacs.002 being the operational standard.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "The ISO 20022 deadline has slipped before. That is the historical record."
2. Reframe as past-tense fact. The November 2025 deadline held. SWIFT's coexistence period ended. Structured pacs.002 rejection codes are now live across eleven thousand plus member institutions. Banks that migrated are not unwinding. The ISO 20022 migration window is a door that has opened — future slippage affects the pace of adoption, not the fact of the data environment.
3. State the second force function. AMLD6 Article 10 criminal liability for legal persons is independent of ISO 20022 timing. Even if ISO 20022 adoption slows, AMLD6 is forcing Tier 2/3 banks to build investigatory hold discrimination infrastructure — which LIP provides. Two independent drivers hedge the "why now" argument.
4. Name the milestone. No external milestone required — the deadline has already held. The resolution is that forward slippage is now behind the curve, not ahead of it. If a regulator reopens the question, the appropriate response is to update the appendix-numbers.md timeline field.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "The deadline won't slip again" (overclaim)
- ❌ "ISO 20022 is inevitable" (vague)
- ❌ "Even if it slips we can wait" (weak — give the second force function)

**Resolution Milestone:** Deadline holds (has held as of November 2025) OR replacement regulatory driver identified (AMLD6 Article 10 investigatory hold infrastructure demand).

**Investor Intuition target:** "The door is open. Even if ISO 20022 had stalled, AMLD6 is the independent force function. The 'why now' argument is structurally hedged."

**Drill linkage:** Q-MKT-02 (Warm — "Why now — what changed?") and Q-MKT-15 (Probing — "ISO 20022 deadlines have slipped before. What if November 2025 slips again?") Both confirmed in drill.md.

## B-MKT-04 — AMLD7 could re-score unit economics

**Honest Truth:** AMLD7 text is not finalised. If the proposed seventh Anti-Money Laundering Directive widens investigatory hold definitions, the BLOCK-class exclusion list expands, and the Class B addressable pool shrinks. That would reduce TAM and potentially compress fee volumes on remaining bridgeable flows.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "AMLD7 is a real regulatory risk. Text is not finalised. If investigatory hold definitions widen, our addressable bridge volume falls."
2. Quantify the scenario. Current addressable bridge volume is approximately six hundred thirty billion dollars ($630B). A material AMLD7 expansion could reduce this toward four hundred billion dollars or lower, depending on which hold types are reclassified. The reduction is additive to the existing exclusion list, not structural to the business model.
3. Reframe as compliance gain, not compliance exposure. The BLOCK-class gate in LIP is designed to expand — adding codes does not break the architecture. Any AMLD7 reclassification makes LIP's hold-type discriminator more valuable as compliance infrastructure, even as it shrinks the bridgeable pool. The bank's regulatory exposure falls, which strengthens the AML officer's buy signal. Fee floor at three hundred basis points and the Tier 2/3 private-counterparty extension still hold on the remaining volume.
4. Name the milestone. AMLD7 final text published is the resolution event. Until then, the business model is calibrated to the AMLD6 regime. The approach is monitoring, not speculative redesign — REX watches the EU legislative pipeline and will flag material deltas before they are enacted.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "AMLD7 won't matter" (unsupported)
- ❌ "We can absorb the hit" (vague)
- ❌ "AMLD7 is years away" (hand-wave; legislative calendar is closer than that)

**Resolution Milestone:** AMLD7 final text published — scenario math updates from speculative to applied.

**Investor Intuition target:** "They've priced the downside scenario. The compliance gate expands rather than breaks. Fee economics survive the shrinkage."

**Drill linkage:** Q-MKT-22 (Crushing — "AMLD7 text changes investigatory hold definitions. Your Class B pool shrinks by half. Argue."). Confirmed in drill.md.

## B-MKT-05 — Incumbents (JPM, BNY) have internal liquidity solutions

**Honest Truth:** JPMorgan handles bridge lending internally for listed counterparties via US7089207B1. BNY Mellon operates deep correspondent custody and clearing infrastructure. Both are capable of extending their internal facilities. LIP's wedge depends on the Tier 2/3 private-counterparty gap holding.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "JPMorgan and BNY have incumbency in Tier 1. They are real competitors and capable internal builders."
2. Reframe as structural gap, not strategy gap. US7089207B1 requires observable equity market data — private counterparties have none. Extending JPMorgan's patent to cover Tier 2/3 private companies requires a structurally new model: Damodaran industry-beta for mid-tier, Altman Z' thin-file for bottom-tier. That is LIP's Tier 2/3 private-counterparty extension — it is not an incremental step from their prior art. BNY has no published product addressing real-time failure classification conditioned on pacs.002.
3. State the licensing-over-build case. Even if JPMorgan or BNY built an internal equivalent, the two-step classification + conditional offer mechanism is patent-protected. An internal build infringes Independent Claim 1 or requires designing around a specific technical gate. Licensing is more efficient than a multi-year design-around. Trade-secret calibration on Damodaran and Altman Z' compounds the build cost.
4. Name the milestone. Pilot bank validation against a Tier 2/3 corridor — resolving whether the wedge holds in practice. If a Tier 2 bank signs a pilot and volumes validate, the incumbent counter-move is defensive, not offensive.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "Incumbents are too slow to move" (complacent)
- ❌ "They'll never build this" (unsupported)
- ❌ "We have a better product" (weak positioning)

**Resolution Milestone:** Pilot bank validation on Tier 2/3 corridors — the wedge is empirically confirmed or empirically refuted.

**Investor Intuition target:** "The patent is a real barrier. The Tier 2/3 extension is a structurally new model their prior art can't reach. Incumbent build is more expensive than a licence."

## B-MKT-06 — TAM composition gap

**Honest Truth:** Thirty-one point six trillion dollars ($31.6T) is the TAM anchor. Most of that is not bridgeable. Half of disrupted volume (by code-category count) is permanently excluded under BLOCK-class investigatory holds. Sub-minimum principals are excluded. The addressable bridge volume — approximately six hundred thirty billion dollars ($630B) — is an order of magnitude below the headline.

**Structured Answer:**
1. Acknowledge clean. "TAM alone is not the market. Half of disrupted volume is permanently blocked. Sub-minimum payments are excluded."
2. Walk the exclusions explicitly. Thirty-one point six trillion dollar TAM. Four percent midpoint failure rate produces one point two seven trillion dollars ($1.27T) disrupted. Exclude the eight BLOCK-class investigatory hold codes (DNOR, CNOR, RR01–RR04, AG01, LEGL). Exclude sub-minimum principals below seven hundred thousand dollars ($700K) for Class B and one million five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) for Class A. Remaining addressable bridge volume: approximately six hundred thirty billion dollars ($630B).
3. State the fee-pool economics. At three hundred to five hundred basis point fee tiers, six hundred thirty billion dollars addressable at even ten percent capture produces a multi-billion-dollar annual fee pool. BPI captures thirty percent of licensee fee revenue in Phase 1. Per-bank SOM: a Tier 1 bank processes three to five trillion dollars annually in correspondent volume — sixty to one hundred billion dollars of bridge principal per bank per year.
4. Name the milestone. Corridor-level volume analysis published against pilot bank data. First live pacs.002 feed confirms the addressable fraction for that corridor and that bank — the headline number becomes evidentiary rather than derivative.

**Don't-say-this:**
- ❌ "TAM is the starting point" (concede and reframe instead)
