## PHASE 0A — Before Talking to ANYONE

**Objective:** Resolve the IP assignment risk and establish clean separation from RBC employment. **Nothing else proceeds until this phase is complete.**

### Checklist

- [ ] **0A-1. Engage BC IP/Employment Lawyer**
  - **Cost:** $5,000–$10,000
  - **Timeline:** Week 1
  - **Deliverable:** Written opinion on RBC employment agreement IP assignment clause
  - **Scope of engagement:**
    - Review RBC offer letter / employment agreement IP clause
    - Assess scope: does clause cover work done on personal time, personal equipment, unrelated to RBC duties?
    - Assess enforceability under BC employment law
    - Advise on pre-employment conception evidence
    - Recommend resolution strategy (release, negotiation, clean-room, or other)
  - **Selection criteria:** Must specialize in tech company IP / employment law in BC. Referral sources: Law Society of BC directory, BCTIA network, startup-focused law firms (Fasken, Osler, Miller Thomson Vancouver)
  - **DO NOT PROCEED TO ANY OTHER STEP UNTIL THIS OPINION IS RECEIVED**

- [ ] **0A-2. Assemble Pre-Employment Conception Evidence**
  - **Cost:** $50–$100 (notarization)
  - **Timeline:** Week 1 (parallel with 0A-1)
  - **Deliverable:** Notarized evidence package
  - **Gather and notarize:**
    - Personal notes, sketches, or documents about LIP concepts from **before January 12, 2026**
    - Google search history showing research into cross-border payment failures
    - Text messages or emails discussing the idea with friends/family
    - Earlier git repositories, code experiments, or prototypes (even partial)
    - Book purchases, course enrollments, or conference attendance related to the domain
    - Domain name research or registration attempts
    - Any dated electronic records (cloud storage timestamps, email dates)
  - **Why this matters:** The RBC IP clause covers works "conceived during employment." Evidence of prior conception is the strongest defense. The IP lawyer will assess this evidence as part of their opinion.
  - **IMPORTANT:** Do this NOW, while memories are fresh and evidence is accessible. Digital evidence can be lost or overwritten.

- [ ] **0A-3. Resign from RBC**
  - **Cost:** Loss of salary (offset by operating buffer in F&F raise)
  - **Timeline:** Week 2–3 (after receiving IP lawyer opinion, or concurrent if opinion is favourable in preliminary assessment)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Written resignation letter (keep brief and professional; do NOT mention BPI or any business plans)
    - Confirm notice period (typically 2 weeks for non-executive roles)
    - Return all RBC property, equipment, and credentials
    - Confirm no non-compete clause (or understand its scope and duration)
    - Confirm no non-solicitation clause applicable to RBC clients
  - **CRITICAL:** Do NOT discuss BPI, the patent, or any business plans with ANY RBC employee (current or former) before, during, or after resignation, until after the IP lawyer has provided their opinion AND resignation is complete
  - **CRITICAL:** Do NOT access, copy, or retain any RBC proprietary information, systems, or data

- [ ] **0A-4. Observe Any Post-Employment Restrictions**
  - **Timeline:** Per RBC agreement (typically 0–12 months)
  - **Action:** IP lawyer will advise on any post-employment non-compete or non-solicitation obligations and whether they restrict BPI's planned activities

### Phase 0A Gate

**Proceed to Phase 0B ONLY when:**
- [x] IP lawyer opinion received (written)
- [x] Opinion is favourable (or clean-room strategy identified)
- [x] Resignation submitted (or resignation timing confirmed with IP lawyer)
- [x] Pre-employment evidence assembled and notarized

**If IP lawyer opinion is unfavourable:** STOP. Reassess entire strategy with IP lawyer. Options include: (a) negotiate release with RBC, (b) clean-room reimplementation, (c) pivot approach. Do NOT proceed with F&F fundraising until a viable path is identified.

## PHASE 0B — Legal Foundation

**Objective:** Incorporate BPI, file patent, execute IP transfer, establish corporate infrastructure. **All steps must complete before any SAFE is signed.**

### Checklist

- [ ] **0B-1. Incorporate BPI under CBCA (Federal)**
  - **Cost:** $2,000–$4,000 (lawyer) + $200 (filing fee)
  - **Timeline:** Week 3–4 (10–15 business days for Corporations Canada processing)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Articles of Incorporation
    - Corporate bylaws
    - Initial board resolution (appointing founder as sole director and officer)
    - Share structure: dual class
      - **Common shares** (voting, unlimited) — founder holds these
      - **Blank-cheque preferred shares** (terms set at issuance) — for future investors
  - **Why CBCA (not Delaware):**
    - Required for S.85 tax-deferred IP transfer
    - Required for SR&ED enhanced ITC (35% rate as CCPC)
    - Required for IRAP eligibility
    - Simpler NI 45-106 compliance (avoids dual US/Canadian securities law)
    - $2K–$4K vs. $5K–$10K for Delaware + ongoing franchise tax
    - Can flip to Delaware at Series A if US institutional investors require it

- [ ] **0B-2. Set Up 15% ESOP Pool**
  - **Cost:** Included in incorporation legal fees
  - **Timeline:** Week 3–4 (concurrent with incorporation)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Board resolution reserving 15% of common shares for equity incentive plan
    - Stock option plan document (can be template at this stage)
  - **Rationale:** Standard for Pre-Seed. Pre-Seed investors expect the ESOP to be established before their round, not created from their dilution.

- [ ] **0B-3. File Provisional Patent Application (US + CA)**
  - **Cost:** $15,000–$25,000 + filing fees (~$1,600 USPTO + $400 CIPO)
  - **Timeline:** Week 3–4 (parallel with incorporation; can file before incorporation if filing in founder's name with subsequent assignment)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - US provisional patent application (USPTO) — establishes **priority date**
    - Canadian priority filing (CIPO)
    - Receipt / filing confirmation with application numbers
  - **Scope:** P1 — core bridging methodology (BIC-pair failure detection, CVA-based pricing, auto-settlement on UETR)
  - **Patent lawyer selection:** Must be registered USPTO patent agent/attorney with fintech experience. Separate from IP/employment lawyer (different specialization).
  - **IMPORTANT:** Patent claims must NOT include prohibited language. No references to AML, SAR, OFAC, SDN, or sanctions screening in patent claims. The patent covers liquidity bridging, not compliance monitoring.

- [ ] **0B-4. Execute IP Assignment (Founder → BPI)**
  - **Cost:** Included in corporate lawyer fees + $500–$1,500 (assignment filing fees)
  - **Timeline:** Week 4–5 (after incorporation; within 30 days of incorporation)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - IP Assignment Agreement (founder assigns all IP to BPI)
    - Board resolution documenting IP transfer and share issuance
    - Patent Assignment filing with USPTO (within 30 days; 4–6 weeks processing)
    - Patent Assignment filing with CIPO
  - **This is the legal transfer of title.** The S.85 election (0B-5) handles the tax treatment.

- [ ] **0B-5. File S.85 Rollover (T2057)**
  - **Cost:** $3,000–$8,000 (accountant)
  - **Timeline:** Week 4–5 (concurrent with IP assignment)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Form T2057 (Election on Disposition of Property by a Taxpayer to a Taxable Canadian Corporation) — signed by BOTH founder and BPI
    - Supporting documentation (description of property, elected amount, cost basis)
  - **Elected amount:** $1 (minimum) — defers all capital gains tax on IP transfer
  - **Accountant selection:** Must be S.85-qualified. Ideally also SR&ED-qualified (two-fer engagement saves future costs).
  - **DEADLINE:** Earlier of (a) founder's T1 return due date, or (b) BPI's first T2 return due date. **Missing this deadline costs $100/month penalty (max $8,000) or denial of election.**
  - **Capital gains rate uncertainty:** Parliament prorogation (Jan 2025) left the inclusion rate uncertain (1/2 or 2/3). Accountant must confirm current rate. This doesn't change the need for the election — it changes the magnitude of the tax saved.

- [ ] **0B-6. Open Corporate Bank Account**
  - **Cost:** $0–$30/month
  - **Timeline:** Week 4 (immediately after incorporation)
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Business chequing account in BPI's name
    - Separate from all personal accounts
  - **CRITICAL:** Never commingle personal and business funds. Required for: corporate veil protection, S.85 integrity, SR&ED defensibility, investor trust.

- [ ] **0B-7. D&O Insurance**
  - **Cost:** $2,000–$5,000/year
  - **Timeline:** Week 4–5 (before any SAFE is signed)
  - **Deliverable:** Directors & Officers liability insurance policy
  - **Why before SAFEs:** If any F&F investor later claims misrepresentation, D&O covers defense costs. This is the founder's personal financial protection. The cost is trivial relative to the risk.

- [ ] **0B-8. Founder Reverse Vesting Agreement**
  - **Cost:** Included in corporate lawyer fees
  - **Timeline:** Week 5–6
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Reverse vesting agreement: 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
    - Credit for pre-incorporation work (6+ months of development)
    - 82(b)-equivalent election (if applicable under Canadian tax law — accountant to advise)
  - **Why:** Pre-Seed investors will require founder vesting. Establishing it now, with credit for prior work, is better than negotiating it under pressure at Pre-Seed.

- [ ] **0B-9. Founder Employment Agreement with BPI**
  - **Cost:** Included in corporate lawyer fees
  - **Timeline:** Week 5–6
  - **Deliverables:**
    - Employment agreement: $0 salary until Pre-Seed closes
    - Standard IP assignment clause (all future work belongs to BPI)
    - Non-compete with BPI (standard for founder-CEO)
  - **Why:** Creates clean legal break. "I resigned from RBC on [date]. I incorporated BPI on [date]. All work after incorporation is under BPI's employment agreement." No gap in IP chain of title.

## PHASE 0D — F&F Round Execution

**Objective:** Execute SAFE agreements with qualifying F&F investors. File regulatory reports.

### Checklist

- [ ] **0D-1. Identify F&F Prospects**
  - **Timeline:** Week 6 (can begin informally earlier, but NO substantive discussions before NDA)
  - **Target:** 8–12 prospects to close 5–8 investors
  - **Qualifying criteria (NI 45-106 s.2.5):**
    - Family members (spouse, parent, grandparent, sibling, child, grandchild)
    - Family-in-law (same relations to spouse)
    - Close personal friends (genuine, long-standing personal relationship)
    - Close business associates (prior business dealings sufficient to assess trustworthiness)
  - **IMPORTANT:** Do NOT approach anyone who does not clearly qualify under s.2.5. When in doubt, consult corporate lawyer.

- [ ] **0D-2. NDA Conversations**
  - **Timeline:** Week 6–8
  - **Process per prospect:**
    1. Informal expression of interest ("I'm starting a company and raising a small round — would you be interested in learning more?")
    2. If interested: sign NDA
    3. After NDA: share investor package (executive summary, SAFE terms, risk disclosure)
    4. Answer questions; provide time for independent legal/financial advice
    5. If proceeding: execute SAFE + risk disclosure + Form 45-106F5

- [ ] **0D-3. SAFE Execution**
  - **Timeline:** Week 7–10
  - **Per investor:**
    1. Confirm investor qualifies under NI 45-106 s.2.5
    2. Investor signs: SAFE, Risk Disclosure acknowledgment, Form 45-106F5
    3. Company signs: SAFE, Risk Disclosure company acknowledgment
    4. Investor transfers funds to BPI corporate bank account
    5. Company issues SAFE confirmation to investor
  - **Minimum check:** $10,000
  - **Maximum check:** $50,000
  - **Side letter:** Offered to investors contributing $25,000+

- [ ] **0D-4. File Reports of Exempt Distribution with BCSC**
  - **Timeline:** Within 10 days of EACH SAFE execution
  - **Per filing:**
    - Complete Form 45-106F1 (Report of Exempt Distribution)
    - Attach: issuer information, distribution details, investor information, exemption relied upon
    - Pay filing fee (~$100)
    - File electronically via BCSC e-filing system

- [ ] **0D-5. Update Corporate Records**
  - **Timeline:** Within 30 days of round close
  - **Actions:**
    - Update shareholder register (note SAFE holders as contingent equity)
    - Update cap table
    - Board resolution acknowledging SAFE issuances
    - File annual return with Corporations Canada (if applicable)

### Phase 0D Gate

**F&F Round complete when:**
- [x] 5–8 investors have executed SAFEs
- [x] Total raised is $75,000–$150,000
- [x] All Form 45-106 reports filed with BCSC
- [x] All funds received in BPI corporate bank account
- [x] Corporate records updated

## Jurisdiction Decision: CBCA (Federal) — Not Delaware

| Factor | CBCA Federal | Delaware C-Corp |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| S.85 rollover | **YES** — required for tax-deferred IP transfer | NO — not available |
| SR&ED / CCPC eligibility | **YES** — 35% enhanced ITC rate | NO — not a CCPC |

| F&F securities law | Straightforward NI 45-106 | Must comply with BOTH US + Canadian law |
| Legal cost | $2K–$4K | $5K–$10K + annual franchise tax + registered agent |
| Future US VC compatibility | Flip to Delaware at Series A if needed | Already there |

**Decision: CBCA now. Flip at Series A if US institutional investors require it.**

## Beyond-the-Edges Recommendations

### R1: SR&ED + IRAP Bridge Strategy
BPI's ML development qualifies for SR&ED tax credits (35% enhanced ITC on first $6M as CCPC). Combined with IRAP ($75K–$200K first award), non-dilutive funding can offset 60%+ of R&D costs. Apply for IRAP immediately after incorporation. The tax accountant engaged for S.85 should also be SR&ED-qualified — two-fer engagement.

### R2: Pre-Employment Evidence Gathering — DO THIS NOW
Before resigning, gather and NOTARIZE any evidence of LIP conception before January 12, 2026: personal notes, Google search history, texts/emails about the idea, earlier git repos, book purchases, domain research. Cost: $50–$100 for notarization. Value: incalculable.

### R3: Clean Room Reimplementation (If IP Counsel Recommends)
After resignation, rebuild the codebase from scratch in a new repository. The existing PRKT2026 repo becomes "prior work product" NOT transferred to BPI. BPI's codebase is rebuilt from the patent specification. Cost: 2–4 weeks with AI assistance (Ford model). This creates an airtight IP provenance chain.

### R4: Founder Employment Agreement with BPI
After incorporation, sign an employment agreement with BPI ($0 salary until Pre-Seed). Creates clean break: "I resigned from RBC, incorporated BPI, all subsequent work is under BPI's employment agreement." No gap between S.85 IP transfer and future work.

### R5: Monthly Investor Updates from Day 1
One-paragraph monthly email to all F&F investors. Builds trust, creates documentary record supporting Pre-Seed narrative, trains the founder for institutional investor communication discipline.

### R6: Separate Finances Immediately
Corporate bank account on Day 1 of incorporation. Never commingle personal and business funds. Required for: corporate veil, S.85 integrity, SR&ED defensibility, investor trust.

### R7: Do NOT Discuss BPI with ANY RBC Employee
No NDA to anyone currently at RBC. No BPI conversations with current or former RBC employees until after IP lawyer opinion AND resignation.

### R8: D&O Insurance Before SAFEs
$2K–$5K/year. If any F&F investor later claims misrepresentation, D&O covers defense costs. This is the founder's personal financial protection.
