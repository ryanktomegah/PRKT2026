### 1.1 Why 5–8 Investors

| Investor Count | Issue |
|----------------|-------|
| **< 5** | Concentration risk. A single $50K investor at $2M cap holds 2.5% and has disproportionate implicit governance weight. If one investor has a dispute, it could block future rounds. |
| **5–8** | Optimal. Diversified risk. Average check $10K–$25K. No single investor has outsized influence. Cap table stays clean for Pre-Seed. |
| **> 10** | Cap table bloat. Each additional line on the cap table slows future institutional due diligence. Pre-Seed investors want to see a clean table, not 15 F&F entries. |
| **> 50** | Loses private issuer status under NI 45-106 (max 50 beneficial holders excluding employees). |

### 1.2 Implied Dilution at Pre-Seed Conversion

| F&F Raise | F&F Ownership at Conversion ($2M cap) | Impact on Founder |
|-----------|---------------------------------------|-------------------|
| $75,000 | 3.6% | Minimal dilution. Founder retains ~81% post-Pre-Seed (vs. 85% pre-ESOP → 68% per financial model) |
| $100,000 | 4.8% | Moderate. Well within institutional investor comfort zone |
| $150,000 | 7.0% | Upper bound. Still manageable but approaching the limit where Pre-Seed investors start questioning early dilution |

## 3. Canadian Securities Law Compliance

### 3.1 Exemption: NI 45-106, Section 2.5

The Friends, Family and Business Associates exemption permits the Company to sell securities without a prospectus to specific categories of individuals related to the Company's directors, executive officers, or control persons.

**Qualifying Investor Categories:**

| Category | Description | Evidence Required |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Director / Officer / Control Person** | The founder (as sole director and officer of BPI) | Corporate records |
| **Family Member** | Spouse, parent, grandparent, brother, sister, child, or grandchild of a director/officer/control person | Relationship documentation |
| **Family-in-Law** | Parent, grandparent, brother, sister, child, or grandchild of the **spouse** of a director/officer/control person | Relationship documentation |
| **Close Personal Friend** | A person who knows the director/officer/control person well enough **and for a long enough period** to be in a position to assess their capabilities and trustworthiness | Signed acknowledgment describing the nature and duration of the relationship |
| **Close Business Associate** | A person who has had sufficient prior business dealings with the director/officer/control person to be in a position to assess their capabilities and trustworthiness | Signed acknowledgment describing the nature and history of the business relationship |

### 3.2 Critical Compliance Requirements

**What IS a "Close Personal Friend":**
- Someone who has known the founder personally (not just professionally) for a sufficient period
- The relationship must be close enough that the person can assess the founder's capabilities and trustworthiness
- Examples: long-time friends, university roommates, people the founder has maintained a genuine personal relationship with over years

**What is NOT a "Close Personal Friend":**
- A Facebook or LinkedIn connection
- Someone met at a networking event
- A professional contact the founder knows only in a business context
- Anyone the founder is approaching primarily because they have money to invest

**Per-Investor Requirements:**
1. **Form 45-106F5** — Each investor must sign the Risk Acknowledgment form BEFORE investing
2. The form must identify the specific category under which the investor qualifies
3. For "close personal friend" or "close business associate," the form must describe the relationship

**Post-Closing Filing Requirements:**
1. **Report of Exempt Distribution** — Must be filed with the BC Securities Commission (BCSC) within **10 days** of each distribution (i.e., each SAFE execution)
2. Filing fee applies (currently $100 per report)
3. The report must include: issuer information, details of the distribution, investor information, and the exemption relied upon

### 3.3 Restrictions and Limitations

| Restriction | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **No Commission / Finder's Fees** | The Company may NOT pay any commission, finder's fee, or referral fee to anyone for introducing investors under s.2.5 |
| **Indefinite Hold Period** | Securities issued under s.2.5 in BC are subject to an **indefinite hold period** — investors cannot resell without a further exemption or prospectus |
| **Private Issuer Status** | The Company must maintain private issuer status: maximum 50 beneficial holders of securities (excluding employees and former employees) |
| **No General Solicitation** | The Company may NOT advertise or publicly solicit investors. All approaches must be direct, personal, and based on pre-existing relationships |
| **Disclosure Obligations** | While no prospectus is required, the Company must not make any misrepresentation to investors. The Investor Risk Disclosure document should be provided to all investors |

### 3.4 Filing Timeline

```
Day 0:    SAFE executed with Investor
Day 1–10: File Report of Exempt Distribution with BCSC
          (form available at bcsc.bc.ca)
Day 120:  First annual summary letter due (if fiscal year ends within 120 days)
```

## 4.3 Platform Vision — What F&F Investors Are Actually Buying

The $2M valuation cap values BPI at its **bridge-lending-only floor**. Here is what that actually means for an F&F investor.

The Revenue Projection Model conservatively projects $226M–$863M in Year 10 revenue from bridge lending alone — a single product line. That single product supports a $1.5B–$5.6B enterprise value at IPO multiples. At a $2M cap, F&F investors are buying in at roughly **0.04–0.13%** of the projected IPO enterprise value. That is already an extraordinary entry point.

But bridge lending is only product #1 of 7.

BPI's 15-patent portfolio, with approximately 32 years of coverage, protects seven distinct revenue categories:

| # | Product | Patent | What It Does (Plain Language) |
|---|---------|--------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | Bridge Lending | P1/P2 | When a cross-border payment fails, BPI automatically lends money to cover the gap until the payment settles. The bank pays BPI a fee. |
| 2 | Platform Licensing | P3 | BPI licenses its technology to large payment companies (like Finastra or FIS), who integrate it into their systems and distribute it to hundreds of banks — without BPI needing to sign each bank individually. |
| 3 | Pre-Emptive Facility | P4 | BPI predicts which payments will fail *before they fail* and pre-positions money to cover the gap automatically. Sold as a monthly subscription to corporate treasurers. |
| 4 | Supply Chain Protection | P5 | When one payment fails in a supply chain, it can cascade — Supplier A can't pay Supplier B, who can't pay Supplier C. BPI detects the cascade and intervenes at the optimal point. |
| 5 | Tokenized Receivables | P7 | BPI turns bridge loans into digital tokens that institutional investors can buy, creating a marketplace. This means unlimited capital — BPI doesn't need to fund the loans itself. |
| 6 | AI Treasury Agent | P8 | A fully autonomous AI system that manages a company's payment monitoring, currency hedging, and liquidity — like a virtual CFO for mid-market companies. Monthly subscription. |
| 7 | Regulatory Data | P10 | BPI's system sees payment failure patterns across multiple banks simultaneously. This data is uniquely valuable to financial regulators (OSFI, FCA, ECB), who cannot produce it themselves. Sold as an annual subscription. |

**What this means for F&F investors:**

The conservative estimate for all seven product streams at Year 10 is **$486M–$1.87B in annual revenue**, supporting an enterprise value of **$2.4B–$15B** at standard fintech multiples. The platform model (adding just 2–3 additional products) reaches $500M–$1.2B at Year 10.

An F&F investor at a $2M cap is buying into a company that:
- Has a **proven, working prototype** with 1,410+ passing tests across its full technical pipeline
- Holds a **20-year foundational patent** covering the core system (to be filed)
- Has **13 additional continuation patents** planned, covering products 2–7 and beyond
- Addresses a **$194.6 trillion market** (FXC Intelligence 2024) — the total cross-border payment market
- Can generate revenue from product #1 (bridge lending) with **zero lending capital** in Phase 1

The F&F round funds the legal prerequisites (IP lawyer, patent filing, incorporation) that convert this working prototype into a patent-protected company. The $2M cap prices BPI at its pre-patent, pre-incorporation, single-product floor. The actual opportunity — a 7-product platform with a 15-patent moat — is what F&F investors are buying at a discount to.

**This is NOT a change to the SAFE terms.** The cap remains $2M. The discount remains 20%. What changes is the context: F&F investors should understand they are not merely investing in a bridge lending startup. They are investing, at the deepest possible discount, in a payment intelligence platform whose patent portfolio covers seven distinct multi-billion-dollar markets.

## 5. Convertible Note Alternative (Side Option)

Per R5 from the fundraising architecture, F&F investors may be offered the **choice** of SAFE or convertible note with identical economic terms. Some investors (particularly older family members) may be more comfortable with a debt instrument.

| Term | SAFE | Convertible Note |
|------|------|-----------------|
| **Legal nature** | Equity-like instrument | Debt instrument |
| **Valuation cap** | $2,000,000 | $2,000,000 |
| **Discount** | 20% | 20% |
| **Interest** | None | 2% simple (nominal) |
| **Maturity** | 24 months | 24 months |
| **Conversion trigger** | Equity Financing ≥ $500K | Equity Financing ≥ $500K |
| **At maturity** | Majority vote: extend or convert at cap | Principal + interest due (or convert at cap) |
| **Investor psychology** | "I invested in the future" | "The company owes me money" |
| **Recommendation** | **Default option** | Offer if investor prefers |

Both instruments convert identically at Pre-Seed. The convertible note adds a nominal interest obligation ($200/year on a $10K note) but provides psychological comfort for investors unfamiliar with SAFEs.

## 6. Side Letter for $25K+ Checks

Investors contributing $25,000 or more may receive a side letter offering:

| Right | Detail | Cost to Company |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Enhanced Information Rights** | Quarterly (not annual) summary updates | Minimal — founder already sending monthly updates |
| **MFN Protection** | Already included in standard SAFE | $0 |
| **Pro-Rata Right in Pre-Seed** | Right to invest up to their pro-rata share in the Pre-Seed round | $0 — just preserves their percentage |
| **Consultation Right** | Founder will make reasonable efforts to discuss major strategic decisions informally | No governance impact |

These rights cost nothing and make larger investors feel appropriately valued without creating governance complications.
