## SECTION 1: GTM Philosophy

**The governing principle:** This is not SaaS. This is not B2C. This is the most complex B2B sale in the world: a venture-stage company selling a patent-protected AI system to a Tier 1 bank's risk-averse committee process. Everything in this playbook flows from four truths:

**Truth 1 — Banks buy outcomes, not technology.**
Never lead with the algorithm. Lead with the revenue line item that appears on the Transaction Banking P&L. "Your corporate clients will stop calling in payment emergencies" is better than "we have a 7-component ML pipeline."

**Truth 2 — Fear is the primary GTM emotion.**
Banks move on fear of competitive disadvantage, not excitement about innovation. The question that opens every door: "Are you comfortable watching a competitor bank offer real-time payment bridging to your corporate clients while you offer them an overdraft and a 2-day wait?"

**Truth 3 — Phase 1 is the only ask in the first conversation.**
Zero capital risk for the bank. Zero capital required from BPI. 70% fee share for the bank. This ask is structurally unrejectable. Don't mention Phase 2 until Phase 1 is live. Don't mention Phase 3 until Phase 2 is in negotiation.

**Truth 4 — The founder's RBC background is the most powerful GTM asset.**
"I sat at the desk where these calls came in. I built the system that would have prevented every one of them." This is the opening sentence of every pitch, every SIBOS talk, every press release. Framed as post-resignation external vendor — never as built-at-RBC.

### The Challenger Sale Applied

Every bank conversation follows: **Teach → Tailor → Take Control**

- **Teach:** What they don't know — the invisible $118.5B problem and that they're losing clients in silence
- **Tailor:** Quantify it for THIS bank's specific payment volumes
- **Take Control:** Propose the PoC ("Let me run this against your historical failures — no commitment required")

## SECTION 6: The PoC → PoV → Pilot Progression

Banks don't sign 6-month pilot agreements cold. There is a proven progression that removes risk at each stage:

### Stage 0 — Proof of Concept (PoC): 2–4 weeks

- **Cost to bank:** $0. No contract required. Under NDA only.
- **What BPI does:** Deploys demo environment against a sample of the bank's historical (anonymized) payment data — or against BPI's synthetic corpus if bank isn't ready to share data.
- **What BPI shows:** Classification accuracy on known historical failures; pricing for payments that did fail; auto-repayment simulation.
- **What bank decides:** "Does this system actually work?"
- **BPI's ask after PoC:** "Let us run a PoV — no loans funded, just observation mode, 8 weeks."
- **Why this works:** Zero risk, zero commitment. Banks can say yes to this without procurement.

### Stage 1 — Proof of Value (PoV): 4–8 weeks

- **Cost to bank:** $0. Under NDA + preliminary data sharing agreement.
- **What BPI does:** Connects to the bank's live pacs.002 stream in read-only observation mode. No loans funded. Generates a "shadow P&L" — what the bank would have earned if LIP had been live.
- **What BPI shows:** "In the past 8 weeks, your payment stream had X bridgeable failures totaling $Y principal. At 30% fee share, you would have earned $Z."
- **What bank decides:** "Is this revenue opportunity real and material for us?"
- **BPI's ask after PoV:** "Based on the PoV data, here is the LOI for the 6-month pilot."
- **Why this works:** The bank has done their own math with their own live data. BPI doesn't need to sell the revenue opportunity — the bank's own payment stream sells it.

### Stage 2 — Pilot: 6 months

- **Cost to bank:** $0 upfront. Revenue share on live loans (70% to bank, 30% to BPI).
- **What BPI does:** Full live deployment. Class A and Class C only (Class B gated on EPG-04/05 `hold_bridgeable` API obligation). Volume cap $100M outstanding.
- **Gate for expansion to full book:** After 3 months, review: (a) AUC > 0.85, (b) default rate < 1%, (c) Phase 1 revenue > $500K. If gates met, expand to remaining corridors and remove volume cap.
- **LOI terms:** Non-binding term sheet; 90-day exclusivity option at 35% BPI/65% bank if bank wants to prevent BPI from approaching direct competitors.

### Stage 3 — Full Phase 1 License

- Standard license agreement; Phase 1 economics locked in; transition to Phase 2 discussion begins at 12-month pilot review.

**Why 30% is non-negotiable:**
- Unit Economics canonical (see data discrepancy notice on 15% issue)

- Signal to capital partners: "BPI negotiated 30% from a Tier 1 bank with zero capital deployed" is a fundamentally different signal than "BPI negotiated 15%"
