# BPI License Agreement — EPG-04/05 Clause Package
## Bridgeability Certification API Obligation + Indemnity Structure

*For BPI counsel review. To be incorporated into BPI License Agreement v2.0 and all pilot bank LOIs.*

---

## Background and Purpose

The BPI Liquidity Intermediation Platform ("LIP") processes ISO 20022 payment failure events and determines whether a bridge loan is appropriate. LIP cannot distinguish between failures that are subject to regulatory or compliance holds and failures that are purely technical or procedural. This distinction is material: bridging a payment that is subject to a compliance hold constitutes a regulatory violation under applicable AML/CFT law regardless of whether LIP was aware of the hold.

The clauses below address this by requiring Licensee to expose a binary bridgeability certification signal that LIP can consume without Licensee disclosing any regulated or protected information about the underlying reason for a hold.

---

## Clause 1 — Bridgeability Certification API Obligation

### 1.1 API Requirement

Licensee shall implement and maintain a Bridgeability Certification API ("BC API") conforming to the technical specification set out in Schedule [X] ("BC API Specification"). The BC API shall, for each ISO 20022 payment failure event submitted to LIP, return a Bridgeability Certification Record with the following mandatory fields:

```
hold_bridgeable:    boolean
    True if the payment is, at the time of certification, eligible for a
    bridge loan under Licensee's internal compliance policies.
    False if the payment is subject to any hold that would render a bridge
    loan unlawful or contrary to Licensee's compliance policies.

certified_by:       string
    Unique identifier of the automated compliance system that generated
    this certification. Must be registered with BPI prior to first use
    pursuant to Clause 1.3.

certification_ts:   ISO 8601 datetime (UTC)
    Timestamp at which the certification was generated.

uetr:               string (UUID v4)
    Unique End-to-End Transaction Reference of the payment to which
    this certification applies.
```

### 1.2 Certification Scope

The BC API shall be configured such that `hold_bridgeable: false` is returned for any payment that is, at the time of certification, subject to any of the following:

(a) a freeze, block, or hold imposed by any government authority with jurisdiction over Licensee, including any sanctions authority;
(b) a hold arising from Licensee's internal suspicious activity monitoring, regardless of whether a suspicious activity report has been or will be filed;
(c) a hold arising from Licensee's know-your-customer, know-your-business, or enhanced due diligence processes where such processes have not been completed and confirmed satisfactory;
(d) a court order, injunction, or judicial freeze affecting the originator, beneficiary, or the payment itself;
(e) any hold arising from Licensee's obligations under applicable anti-money laundering, counter-terrorist financing, or sanctions compliance law.

For the avoidance of doubt, Licensee shall not be required to disclose the specific reason for a `hold_bridgeable: false` certification to BPI. The binary signal constitutes the entirety of the required disclosure.

### 1.3 System Registration

Prior to the first issuance of any Bridgeability Certification Record, Licensee shall register with BPI the `certified_by` system identifier and provide BPI with written confirmation that the registered system meets the requirements of Clause 2.

### 1.4 LIP Response to Certification

BPI shall configure LIP to treat any `hold_bridgeable: false` certification as a hard block: no bridge loan offer shall be generated for any payment for which `hold_bridgeable: false` has been received. BPI shall not override or circumvent this block by any means.

## Clause 2 — System Integrity Warranty

### 2.1 Automated Generation Requirement

Licensee warrants that all Bridgeability Certification Records are generated exclusively by the automated compliance system identified in the applicable `certified_by` field, and that no Bridgeability Certification Record is generated by:

(a) manual entry or manual override by any employee, officer, or agent of Licensee;
(b) any automated process that accepts manual input as to the `hold_bridgeable` value;
(c) any system that has not been reviewed and approved by Licensee's compliance function within the preceding twelve (12) months.

### 2.2 Documented Controls

Licensee shall maintain documented controls governing the operation of any system registered under Clause 1.3, including:

(a) change management procedures requiring compliance function approval for any modification to certification logic;
(b) access controls preventing any individual from modifying certification outcomes outside the documented change management process;
(c) an audit log of all certification records generated, retained for a minimum of seven (7) years;
(d) annual review by Licensee's internal audit function of the controls described in this Clause 2.2.

### 2.3 BPI Audit Rights

BPI may, upon thirty (30) days' written notice, request that Licensee provide written evidence of compliance with Clauses 2.1 and 2.2, including the most recent internal audit report relating to the registered system. Licensee may redact from such reports any information subject to legal professional privilege or regulatory confidentiality obligations.

## Clause 3 — Certification Warranty

### 3.1 Warranty

Licensee warrants that, at the time of issuance of any Bridgeability Certification Record with `hold_bridgeable: true`, the payment identified by the `uetr` field in that record is not, to the best of Licensee's knowledge after applying its standard compliance procedures:

(a) subject to any freeze or block described in Clause 1.2(a);
(b) the subject of an active internal hold of the types described in Clauses 1.2(b) through 1.2(e);
(c) otherwise such that the provision of a bridge loan by BPI would constitute a violation of applicable law by BPI.

### 3.2 Knowledge Standard

For the purposes of Clause 3.1, "knowledge" means actual knowledge of Licensee's automated compliance system as reflected in the data inputs available to that system at the time of certification. Licensee does not warrant that its compliance system has access to or has processed information not available to it in the ordinary course of Licensee's compliance operations.

### 3.3 Prospective Holds

If, after issuing a `hold_bridgeable: true` certification, Licensee becomes aware that the relevant payment is or was at the time of certification subject to a hold described in Clause 1.2, Licensee shall notify BPI as promptly as practicable, consistent with Licensee's obligations under applicable law (including, for the avoidance of doubt, any applicable tipping-off prohibition).

## Clause 4 — Indemnification

### 4.1 Bank Indemnity

Licensee shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless BPI and its directors, officers, employees, and agents ("BPI Indemnitees") from and against any and all losses, claims, damages, fines, penalties, enforcement costs, legal fees, and expenses ("Losses") arising out of or relating to:

(a) any Bridgeability Certification Record that Licensee issued with `hold_bridgeable: true` where the relevant payment was, at the time of that certification, subject to a hold described in Clause 1.2; or
(b) any breach by Licensee of the warranties in Clauses 2 or 3.

### 4.2 Conditions on Indemnity

BPI's right to indemnification under Clause 4.1 is conditioned on:

(a) BPI promptly notifying Licensee in writing upon becoming aware of any claim for which indemnification may be sought;
(b) BPI granting Licensee sole control of the defence of such claim (provided that Licensee shall not settle any claim in a manner that imposes any obligation or restriction on BPI without BPI's prior written consent);
(c) BPI providing reasonable cooperation and assistance to Licensee in connection with the defence.

### 4.3 Exclusions

Licensee shall not be obligated to indemnify BPI Indemnitees to the extent that Losses result from:

(a) BPI's failure to implement the hard-block required by Clause 1.4;
(b) BPI's modification of LIP in a manner that causes it to process a payment notwithstanding a `hold_bridgeable: false` certification;
(c) BPI's own negligence, wilful misconduct, or violation of applicable law.

## Negotiation Notes (internal BPI use — remove before sending to bank)

**Point most likely to draw pushback**: Clause 2 (system integrity warranty). Bank's legal team will resist committing to documented controls and audit rights in a commercial agreement. This is the line to hold: without Clause 2, a rogue treasury officer can flip `hold_bridgeable: true` on sanctioned payments and BPI has no contractual basis to enforce against that scenario. The indemnity in Clause 4 is only as good as the warranties it backs.

**Counter to bank pushback on Clause 2**: Frame it as protecting the bank. If Licensee is indemnifying BPI under Clause 4, Licensee needs documented controls to be able to demonstrate in any enforcement action that the mis-certification was anomalous and not systemic. Without controls documentation, Licensee cannot defend itself.

**Revenue argument for Clause 2 resistance**: Without this clause structure, Class B payments stay blocked in LIP permanently. The bank loses all B1 bridge loan revenue that LIP could generate on permitted Class B payments (procedural holds where BPI can legitimately lend). The annual revenue opportunity from B1 unlocking should be quantified before the negotiation and used as the opening frame.

**Liability cap (Clause 4.4)**: BPI position is uncapped for regulatory fines. Practical reality: no large bank will sign an uncapped indemnity. Likely landing point is a cap at some multiple of annual fees for commercial damages, with a carve-out leaving regulatory fines and government enforcement costs uncapped. This is consistent with market practice for AML-related indemnities.
