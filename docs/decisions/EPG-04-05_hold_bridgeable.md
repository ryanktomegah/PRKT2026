## Decision

**Do NOT ask banks for a "hold reason" API.** Asking why a payment is on hold is FATF-prohibited (R.21 tipping-off — disclosing the existence of an internal compliance investigation to anyone, including downstream technology providers, is itself a violation in most jurisdictions).

Instead, the LIP integration requires a single boolean **certification** field per payment, emitted by the bank's compliance system:

```
hold_bridgeable:    boolean   # set by bank's internal compliance system
certified_by:       system_id # bank compliance system identifier
certification_ts:   ISO-8601  # timestamp of certification
```

The bank sets the flag per its own internal policy without disclosing why a hold was raised. LIP **hard-blocks** on `false` and **may offer** on `true`. This mirrors the FATF R.13 correspondent KYC certification structure, which is well-understood by bank compliance teams and FATF examiners.

## Why this design (not the obvious alternative)

The naive integration would have LIP ask "give me the reason code, I'll decide whether to bridge." That puts BPI inside the bank's compliance perimeter, makes BPI a co-controller of compliance data under GDPR Art. 26, exposes BPI to the bank's tipping-off liability, and forces the bank to share information their own internal policies forbid them from sharing. Every one of those is a deal-killer.

Instead, BPI takes a binary signal that the bank's compliance system *already produces internally* and treats it as the gating input. BPI never learns *why*. The bank never has to disclose. Both sides stay on their respective sides of the wall, and the FATF examiner sees a structure that already exists in correspondent banking.

## License Agreement warranties (EPG-05)

The BPI License Agreement must include three warranties from the bank, without which `hold_bridgeable=true` is contractually meaningless and Class B remains permanently block-all:

1. **Certification warranty** — any payment with `hold_bridgeable=true` is not, at certification time, under OFAC/SDN sanctions, an open SAR investigation, a PEP review, a court freeze, or any other hold that would render a bridge unlawful.

2. **System integrity warranty** — the certification is generated by an automated compliance system with documented controls. **No manual override is permitted on the flag.** This warranty is what makes the certification trustworthy at audit time.

3. **Indemnification** — the bank indemnifies BPI for all regulatory fines, enforcement actions, and reputational damages if BPI funds a bridge on `hold_bridgeable=true` and that payment proves to have been under a hold at certification time.

## Negotiation lever for pilot banks

Without warranty #2, BPI cannot trust the flag, so Class B (B1 + B2 sub-classification) stays permanently block-all in code. The bank then loses **all** B1 LIP revenue. This is the entire commercial leverage that makes warranty #2 acceptable to bank legal teams: they want B1 revenue; B1 revenue requires warranty #2; therefore they accept warranty #2.

**Critical sequencing.** This language must appear in the pilot bank Letter of Intent **before** the bank's legal team reviews any draft of the License Agreement. Starting from a deficient LOI structure means months of renegotiation, because once a bank legal team sees a draft without warranty #2, they will assume it is optional.

The Class B unlock (split block-all into B1/B2) is described in [`EPG-19`](EPG-19_compliance_hold_bridging.md) and is purely mechanical once the contractual layer is in place. `class_b_eligible=False` is pre-wired in `LoanOfferExpiry` records (EPG-23) for ARIA's data cut.
