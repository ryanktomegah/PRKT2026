## 1. GENERAL INVESTMENT RISKS

### 1.1 Total Loss of Investment

Investing in early-stage companies is speculative and involves a high degree of risk. **The majority of early-stage companies fail, and investors in such companies frequently lose their entire investment.** You should only invest money that you can afford to lose completely without affecting your financial security or lifestyle.

### 1.2 No Secondary Market

There is no public market for the securities of BPI. There is no guarantee that a market will develop. The securities issued under this SAFE are subject to an **indefinite hold period** under British Columbia securities legislation. You may never be able to sell or transfer your investment.

### 1.3 Indefinite Hold Period

Your investment may be illiquid for an indefinite period. You should be prepared to hold this investment for **5–10 years or longer** before any liquidity event (such as an acquisition or IPO) may occur. Many companies never achieve a liquidity event.

### 1.4 Dilution

Your ownership percentage will be diluted by future fundraising rounds. The Company's current financial model projects the following equity dilution path:

| Round | Projected Pre-Money | Projected Founder Ownership After |
|-------|--------------------|---------------------------------|
| F&F (current) | $2,000,000 | ~85% (pre-ESOP) |
| Pre-Seed | $6,000,000 | ~68% |
| Seed | $18,000,000 | ~55% |
| Series A | $50,000,000–$60,000,000 | ~45% |

F&F investors will be diluted proportionally at each subsequent round. While the SAFE's valuation cap provides downside protection on conversion price, it does not protect against dilution from future rounds.

### 1.5 No Dividend Expectation

BPI does not intend to pay dividends for the foreseeable future. Any returns will depend entirely on a future liquidity event.

## 2. COMPANY-SPECIFIC RISKS

### 2.1 Pre-Incorporation Status

As of the date of this disclosure, BPI has **not yet been incorporated**. The Company is in the process of incorporating under the Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA). This is unusual at the fundraising stage. Proceeds from this round will be used, in part, to fund the incorporation process. Until incorporation is complete, the SAFE is an obligation of the founder personally, not of a corporate entity.

### 2.2 Intellectual Property Assignment Risk — CRITICAL

**This is the most material risk facing the Company and requires your careful attention.**

The founder of BPI is currently employed by the Royal Bank of Canada ("RBC"). The founder's employment agreement with RBC contains a broad intellectual property assignment clause that covers works "conceived, reduced to practice, or developed" during the period of employment.

**Key facts:**

1. The founder's RBC employment began on **January 12, 2026**
2. The git repository containing BPI's technology has a commit history that begins **after** the RBC employment start date
3. This means that, on its face, the entire codebase was developed during the period covered by RBC's IP assignment clause
4. An IP/employment lawyer has **not yet been engaged** to provide an opinion on this clause
5. The founder intends to resign from RBC before incorporating BPI, and the IP lawyer engagement is the **first use of proceeds** from this round

**Possible outcomes of the IP lawyer engagement:**

| Outcome | Likelihood | Impact |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Favourable opinion** — work is outside scope of RBC clause (e.g., done on personal time, personal equipment, unrelated to RBC duties) | Unknown | Proceeds to patent filing and incorporation as planned |
| **Negotiated release** — RBC agrees to release any claim to the IP | Unknown | May involve negotiation time and minor cost |
| **Licence from RBC** — RBC claims ownership but agrees to licence the IP to BPI | Unknown | Ongoing royalty obligation to RBC; reduces BPI margins |
| **Clean-room reimplementation** — IP counsel recommends rebuilding the codebase from scratch after resignation | Unknown | 2–4 week delay; existing repo becomes prior work product not transferred to BPI |
| **Adverse outcome** — RBC asserts ownership and does not agree to release or licence | Unknown | Fundamental threat to BPI's viability. May require abandonment of current technology. |

**The Company cannot assess the probability of any of these outcomes until an IP lawyer has reviewed the specific language of the RBC employment agreement.** The engagement of this lawyer is the highest-priority use of F&F proceeds.

**Pre-employment conception evidence:** The founder is assembling evidence that the core concepts underlying BPI's technology were conceived **before** the RBC employment start date (personal notes, research, communications from before January 12, 2026). The strength and completeness of this evidence will be a key factor in the IP lawyer's opinion.

### 2.3 Solo Founder Risk

BPI has a **single founder** with no co-founder, no employees, and no formal advisory board. This creates extreme key-person risk:

- If the founder becomes unable to work (illness, accident, personal circumstances), the Company has no one to continue operations
- All technical, business, legal, and financial knowledge resides with one person
- Institutional investors typically prefer founding teams of 2–3 people
- The founder's "Ford model" (AI-augmented solo development) is innovative but unproven at scale

**Mitigation (planned, not yet implemented):** The founder intends to implement a reverse vesting schedule and maintain comprehensive documentation. The Pre-Seed round will fund the first 2–3 hires.

### 2.4 Pre-Revenue Risk

BPI has **zero revenue**. The Company has never processed a real payment, never executed a real bridge loan, and never collected a fee from a customer.

All revenue projections cited in Company materials are internal estimates based on assumptions about:
- Market size ($31.7 trillion annual cross-border B2B payment volume — industry figure, not BPI-specific)
- Failure rates (3–5% — based on BIS/SWIFT GPI data, not BPI-specific)
- Bank adoption rates (assumed, not validated by any bank)
- Fee levels (modeled at 300–700+ bps — theoretical, not market-tested)
- Phase transition timing (assumed, not experienced)

**There is no assurance that any of these projections will be achieved.**

### 2.5 Patent Risk

BPI has **not filed any patent applications**. The Company's business strategy depends on a 15-patent portfolio covering cross-border payment bridging technology. As of this date:

- No provisional patent application has been filed
- No priority date has been established
- A competitor could file a similar patent before BPI
- Patent prosecution is expensive ($100K–$155K for Phase 1 including PCT) and uncertain
- There is no guarantee that any patent will be granted
- Even if granted, patents can be challenged, invalidated, or designed around

The filing of a provisional patent (establishing a priority date) is the second-highest-priority use of F&F proceeds.

### 2.6 Regulatory Risk

BPI's business model involves extending short-term credit (bridge loans) to payees of failed cross-border payments. Depending on the jurisdiction and phase of deployment:

- **Phase 1 (IP licensing):** Minimal regulatory exposure — BPI licenses technology; the bank makes lending decisions
- **Phase 2 (co-lending):** BPI may need to be licensed as a lender in some jurisdictions
- **Phase 3 (BPI-funded lending):** BPI will need lending licences, potentially in multiple jurisdictions

The regulatory landscape for cross-border payment bridging is untested. Regulators may impose requirements that increase BPI's operating costs, restrict its business model, or prevent it from operating in certain markets.

### 2.7 Market Risk — No Bank Has Expressed Interest

No bank has signed a letter of intent, pilot agreement, or any other form of commitment to use BPI's technology. The Company's go-to-market strategy targets Tier 1 banks, which have:

- Long sales cycles (12–24 months is typical for new technology vendors)
- Extensive compliance and vendor due diligence requirements
- Institutional inertia and preference for established vendors
- Internal teams that may attempt to build similar solutions

**There is no assurance that any bank will adopt BPI's technology.**

### 2.8 Technology Risk

BPI's platform was built with significant AI assistance (the "Ford model"). While the system has 1,476 passing tests and a working inference pipeline:

- The system has **never been tested in a production environment**
- It has **never processed a real SWIFT payment message**
- Performance at scale (thousands of concurrent loans) is unproven
- The ML models have been trained and validated on historical/simulated data, not live production data
- Security and compliance audits have not been conducted
- Integration with real bank systems has not been tested

### 2.9 Conversion Risk

If no Equity Financing (minimum $500,000) occurs within the SAFE's 24-month maturity period:

- The SAFE holders may vote to extend the maturity by 12 months or convert at the cap
- If the Company has insufficient assets, the SAFE holders may receive shares worth less than their investment
- There is no guarantee of a future priced round
- In a dissolution event, SAFE holders are paid before common shareholders but after creditors — if the Company has no assets, the payment is zero
